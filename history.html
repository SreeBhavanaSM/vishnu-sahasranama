History

The sacred journey that led to the revelation of the Vishnu Sahasranama.

The great Kurukshetra War had finally ended. The battle that shook kingdoms, families, and generations was over.

The Pandavas emerged victorious, yet the battlefield was covered with loss and sorrow. Victory had come at a terrible price.

Thousands had fallen. Teachers, friends, relatives, and warriors lay silent beneath the evening sky.

Yudhishthira was crowned king of Hastinapura. Yet peace did not enter his heart.

Instead of celebration, he felt guilt and grief. Every victory reminded him of the lives that had been lost.

He wondered whether any throne was worth such suffering. Questions about duty, justice, and life itself troubled him.

Seeking answers, Yudhishthira turned to Sri Krishna.

Krishna knew that the greatest living repository of wisdom still remained on Earth.

Bhishma Pitamaha, lying upon a bed of arrows, still waited for the chosen moment of his departure.

Bhishma possessed the blessing of choosing the moment of his own death.

Though pierced by countless arrows, his mind remained calm and radiant.

Kings, sages, and warriors gathered around him, knowing that his wisdom was beyond compare.

Yudhishthira asked profound questions.

Who is the Supreme Reality? What is the highest refuge? What is the greatest path of Dharma?

How can a human being attain peace? How can one become free from the cycle of suffering?

Bhishma's answer was remarkable.

He taught that remembering and chanting the thousand names of the Supreme Lord was one of the highest spiritual practices.

These names became known as the Vishnu Sahasranama.

The name Vishnu comes from a root meaning "to pervade."

Bhishma describes the Divine as that which exists everywhere, within all beings, and throughout the universe.

The focus is not on sectarian identity, but on the all-pervading Reality itself.

From this sacred conversation emerged one of the most beloved hymns in Indian spiritual tradition.

For centuries, seekers have recited these names for peace, devotion, contemplation, and liberation.

Every name reveals a different facet of the Infinite. Every verse becomes a step inward.

The journey now continues with the Shlokas.
